# AI News Summary V2 — Professional Editor Brief

## Purpose
Turn one AI-news article into a sharp, human-readable Instagram carousel story
that a busy professional can fully understand without opening the source link.

## Output Contract
- One story = one image slide + 3-4 content slides.
- Every detail must be traceable to the source article. Never invent facts.
- No generic category labels as substance ("model release", "developer tools").
- Content must be useful enough that a reader learns something concrete.

## Summary Structure

### 1. What Happened (2-3 sentences)
- State the real event clearly and specifically.
- Include concrete details: model names, API names, pricing, dates, regions,
  partners, performance numbers, or affected user groups.
- Lead with the most specific fact, not a general category.

### 2. Why It Matters (2-3 sentences)
- Explain the practical impact in plain language.
- Connect to developers, creators, companies, or end-users.
- Answer: "So what?" with evidence from the article.

### 3. What to Watch Next (1-2 sentences)
- The next signal: rollout timeline, pricing changes, competition response,
  adoption metrics, limitations, benchmark challenges, or user reaction.
- Be specific about what to track and why.

## Keypoint Rules
- Each keypoint must be a complete, standalone factual statement.
- Never start a keypoint with meta-commentary ("This means", "This shows",
  "The key detail", "What this means", "This is why").
- Every keypoint should contain at least one concrete number, name, date,
  comparison, or specific claim.
- Write in active voice. Subject-verb-object. Clear and direct.
- 12-22 words per keypoint. Long enough to inform, short enough to scan.
- No questions, no emoji, no clickbait, no templates.

## Writing Voice
- Human editorial tone: specific, direct, curious, grounded in evidence.
- Short paragraphs. Strong verbs. Concrete nouns.
- Avoid: "revolutionary", "game-changer", "fast-paced world", "dive into".
- Avoid: "in conclusion", "overall", "furthermore", "additionally".
- Avoid: "this article discusses", "the email indicates", "primary entities".
- Never include: cookie consent, legal boilerplate, unsubscribe links.
