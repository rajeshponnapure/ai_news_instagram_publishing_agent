# Uniqueness Engine
> Loaded by: SKILL.md — MANDATORY for every UI task
> Purpose: Guarantee every UI is visually distinct. No two designs from this agent may look the same.

---

## 🎲 THE UNIQUENESS MANDATE

> **Rule #1:** You may not reuse the same aesthetic direction twice in a row.
> **Rule #2:** You may not use the same font pairing twice in a project session.
> **Rule #3:** You may not use the same primary color family twice in a project session.
> **Rule #4:** You may not use the same layout archetype twice in a session.

Before every design, read the current session context and actively choose something DIFFERENT.

---

## 🧭 AESTHETIC DIRECTION WHEEL

There are **20 distinct aesthetic directions** defined below. Rotate through them.
Pick the one that best fits the brief — and that you have NOT used recently.

---

### 01 — REFINED BRUTALISM
**Character:** Raw power with intentional restraint. Big, heavy type. Stark contrast. Grid-breaking.
**Palette rule:** High contrast — usually black/white + ONE punchy accent
**Typography:** Heavy condensed display (Barlow Condensed, Anton, Bebas Neue) + mono body
**Layout DNA:** Asymmetric grid, oversized numbers/headings, thick borders as design elements
**Avoid:** Rounded corners, gradients, soft shadows
**Best for:** Developer tools, agencies, bold startups, portfolios

---

### 02 — SOFT ORGANICS
**Character:** Natural, breathing, rounded. Feels like it was crafted by hand.
**Palette rule:** Earthy tones — terracotta, sage, cream, warm browns
**Typography:** Humanist serif display + rounded sans body (Fraunces + Nunito)
**Layout DNA:** Uneven spacing, blob shapes, overlapping elements, generous whitespace
**Avoid:** Sharp corners, neons, heavy drop shadows
**Best for:** Wellness, food, artisan brands, personal portfolios, nature products

---

### 03 — DARK FUTURISM
**Character:** Digital, immersive, technical. Feels like a sci-fi control panel.
**Palette rule:** Deep dark bg + neon/electric accent + subtle grid overlays
**Typography:** Geometric sans or mono (Orbitron, Space Mono, Rajdhani)
**Layout DNA:** Dashboard-style grids, glowing elements, status indicators
**Avoid:** Light backgrounds, serif fonts, organic shapes
**Best for:** SaaS products, tech dashboards, gaming, developer tools, crypto

---

### 04 — EDITORIAL LUXURY
**Character:** Magazine-quality. Deliberate whitespace. Typography as art.
**Palette rule:** Near-neutral with one deep jewel tone + warm metallic accent
**Typography:** High-contrast serif (Cormorant, Bodoni Moda) + clean sans body
**Layout DNA:** Asymmetric columns, large image bleed, editorial spacing
**Avoid:** Gradients, neons, busy backgrounds, generic icons
**Best for:** Fashion, luxury products, culture/arts, high-end services

---

### 05 — GEOMETRIC MODERNISM
**Character:** Bauhaus-inspired. Shapes and color as structure.
**Palette rule:** Primary color triad — bold, saturated, equal-weight
**Typography:** Pure geometric sans (Futura-like, Jost, Raleway)
**Layout DNA:** Strict grid, circles/triangles as decoration, color blocks as sections
**Avoid:** Gradients, ornamental fonts, excess rounded corners
**Best for:** Architecture, design studios, branding agencies, art

---

### 06 — GLASSMORPHISM & DEPTH
**Character:** Translucent layers, frosted glass, depth through blur and opacity.
**Palette rule:** Gradient backgrounds (subtle) + white/semi-transparent cards
**Typography:** Clean modern sans (Sora, Plus Jakarta Sans, Manrope)
**Layout DNA:** Floating cards with blur, layered depth, subtle glow effects
**Avoid:** Flat opaque backgrounds, heavy borders, stark contrast
**Best for:** Apps, SaaS, fintech, productivity tools

---

### 07 — RETRO / VINTAGE DIGITAL
**Character:** Nostalgic but polished. Feels like a modernized 80s/90s interface.
**Palette rule:** Muted retro palette — dusty orange, avocado green, warm cream, aged yellow
**Typography:** Retro-inspired display (Bebas Neue, Alfa Slab One) + slab body
**Layout DNA:** Grid lines, decorative borders, badge elements, offset shadows
**Avoid:** Blue-dominant palettes, ultra-clean minimalism
**Best for:** Newsletters, personal projects, entertainment, food/beverage

---

### 08 — HYPER-MINIMAL
**Character:** Almost nothing. Every element earns its place by necessity.
**Palette rule:** Near-monochrome — 2 colors max (usually a near-black + near-white)
**Typography:** One font family, maximum 2 weights
**Layout DNA:** Extreme whitespace, single focal point, invisible grid
**Avoid:** Decorative elements, multiple colors, complex layouts
**Best for:** Documentation, portfolios, content-first products, premium services

---

### 09 — NEO-SKEUOMORPHISM (CLAYMORPHISM)
**Character:** Soft, tactile, 3D-like. Feels physical without being realistic.
**Palette rule:** Pastel base + deeper shade of same hue for shadows
**Typography:** Rounded, friendly (Nunito, Baloo, Fredoka One)
**Layout DNA:** Puffy cards, soft inner shadows, extruded elements
**Avoid:** Flat design, hard edges, dark themes
**Best for:** Consumer apps, games, children's products, fun tools

---

### 10 — TYPOGRAPHIC CHAOS
**Character:** Type IS the design. Wild, expressive, rule-breaking typography.
**Palette rule:** Limited — 1–2 colors max to let type breathe
**Typography:** Mixed sizes, weights, styles — intentionally varied
**Layout DNA:** No standard grid, text at angles, massive size contrasts
**Avoid:** Grid-based layouts, decorative illustration
**Best for:** Music, art, provocative brands, cultural projects, posters

---

### 11 — CORPORATE SOPHISTICATION
**Character:** Trustworthy, polished, confident. A Fortune 500 company redesigned by a top agency.
**Palette rule:** Deep navy or slate + warm accent (gold, amber) + clean white
**Typography:** Authoritative (Neue Haas Grotesk feel: IBM Plex Sans, Hanken Grotesk)
**Layout DNA:** Clean columns, structured hierarchy, data tables done elegantly
**Avoid:** Playfulness, extreme asymmetry, neon accents
**Best for:** Finance, legal, healthcare, B2B SaaS, enterprise

---

### 12 — VIBRANT MAXIMALISM
**Character:** More is more. Loud, proud, visually rich — but intentionally composed.
**Palette rule:** 5+ colors in harmony — think curated "chaos"
**Typography:** Expressive display + contrast body
**Layout DNA:** Dense, layered, every inch designed, illustration + type + color together
**Avoid:** Minimalism, safe choices, neutral backgrounds
**Best for:** Gen-Z brands, entertainment, music, streetwear, social platforms

---

### 13 — AURORA / BOREALIS
**Character:** Flowing gradients, light leaks, ethereal depth. Feels alive.
**Palette rule:** Multi-stop gradients — purples, pinks, blues, greens — deeply saturated
**Typography:** Elegant modern sans (Syne, Outfit, Cabinet Grotesk)
**Layout DNA:** Gradient backgrounds, glow effects, floating blurred orbs as decoration
**Avoid:** Flat opaque colors, heavy borders, serif body text
**Best for:** AI/tech products, creative tools, music/audio apps

---

### 14 — JAPANESE MINIMALISM (WA AESTHETIC)
**Character:** Quiet elegance. Informed by Ma (negative space) and wabi-sabi philosophy.
**Palette rule:** Muted naturals — ink black, bamboo cream, stone gray, moss green
**Typography:** Thin weight sans (Zen Kaku Gothic, Noto Sans) + minimal hierarchy
**Layout DNA:** Extreme negative space, content as meditation, single focal point
**Avoid:** Clutter, bright colors, drop shadows, decorative elements
**Best for:** Mindfulness apps, premium products, photography, architecture

---

### 15 — GLASSPUNK
**Character:** Glassmorphism meets punk energy — translucent but raw and gritty.
**Palette rule:** Dark base + neon accent + frosted overlays
**Typography:** Heavy distressed or industrial display + clean mono
**Layout DNA:** Broken grid + frosted cards + texture overlays
**Avoid:** Soft pastels, clean corporate feel
**Best for:** Music, nightlife, gaming, underground culture

---

### 16 — WARM EDITORIAL
**Character:** Like a beautifully designed book or magazine. Inviting and intellectual.
**Palette rule:** Warm paper tones + ink black + muted single accent
**Typography:** Refined serif (Freight Display, Vollkorn) + humanist sans
**Layout DNA:** Column-based, drop caps, pull quotes, refined typographic detail
**Avoid:** Tech aesthetics, gradients, neon
**Best for:** Publishing, blogs, longform content, culture, education

---

### 17 — DATA BRUTALISM
**Character:** Information architecture as aesthetics. Density is beautiful.
**Palette rule:** Mostly dark with accent-colored data elements
**Typography:** Mono (JetBrains Mono, Fira Code) + data labels everywhere
**Layout DNA:** Dense data tables, inline stats, terminal-feel, zero decoration
**Avoid:** Decorative elements, soft colors, illustration
**Best for:** Developer dashboards, analytics, monitoring, CLI apps

---

### 18 — COTTAGECORE DIGITAL
**Character:** Cozy, handcrafted, nostalgic. Digital version of a handwritten journal.
**Palette rule:** Dusty pastels — lavender, blush, sage, buttercream
**Typography:** Script accents + handwriting-feel serif body
**Layout DNA:** Organic shapes, imperfect borders, soft texture backgrounds
**Avoid:** Sharp precision, dark themes, corporate feel
**Best for:** Journaling apps, creative tools, lifestyle, community products

---

### 19 — ATHLETIC PRECISION
**Character:** Energy, speed, performance. Bold and functional.
**Palette rule:** High-contrast — black + ONE electric accent (lime, red, cyan)
**Typography:** Bold condensed (Impact-like, Barlow Condensed Black)
**Layout DNA:** Diagonal cuts, speed lines, full-bleed, kinetic composition
**Avoid:** Soft fonts, pastel colors, excessive whitespace
**Best for:** Sports, fitness, health tech, gaming, competitive tools

---

### 20 — SACRED GEOMETRY
**Character:** Mathematical beauty. Patterns, repetition, and proportion as design.
**Palette rule:** Deep, jewel-toned with metallic accents (gold, copper, silver)
**Typography:** Geometric precision (Josefin Sans, Poiret One, Cinzel)
**Layout DNA:** Grid-derived decorative patterns, mandala-inspired elements, symmetry
**Avoid:** Chaos, randomness, cartoon-feel
**Best for:** Luxury, spiritual/wellness, architecture, high-concept branding

## 🎯 SELECTION CRITERIA

When choosing an aesthetic direction:

1. **Brief match** — Does this aesthetic serve the actual use case?
2. **Audience match** — Does this aesthetic feel right for who will use it?
3. **Novelty check** — Have I NOT used this recently?
4. **Boldness check** — Is this the most interesting direction that still fits the brief?

> When two directions both fit the brief equally well → choose the bolder, less expected one.
